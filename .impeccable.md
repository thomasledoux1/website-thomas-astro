# Impeccable — design context

## Design Context

### Users

The site serves **other developers** and **hiring managers**. Visitors should quickly understand who you are, read your writing, and see your work. Context is mostly professional browsing (short sessions, scanning for credibility and craft).

### Brand Personality

**Developer · animations · hipster** — technical credibility without corporate stiffness; motion and small flourishes signal craft. The vibe should feel considered and a little distinctive, not generic “portfolio template.”

**Reference direction (not literal copies):**

- **Notion**: Calm structure, content-first layout, lots of breathing room, typography that stays readable at length, subtle hierarchy.
- **Cursor**: Product-feeling polish for developers — crisp UI, purposeful motion, dark-first comfort for long sessions.

**Anti-references:** None specified; avoid looking like a stock SaaS landing or interchangeable AI-generated portfolio.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Minimal** base with **playful** accents (motion, small interactions, unexpected details — not busy decoration).
- **Theme:** Support **light and dark**; **dark is the default** (optimize contrast, surfaces, and accent use for dark first; light mode should feel equally intentional).
- **Color:** No mandatory or forbidden palette specified — choose a cohesive system that stays legible in both themes and meets contrast targets.
- Motion is part of the brand (“animations”); pair with **WCAG 2.1 AA** (contrast, focus, semantics) and **respect `prefers-reduced-motion`** so animation never blocks understanding.

### Design Principles

1. **Dark-first, dual-theme** — Default to dark; light mode is a first-class alternative, not an afterthought.
2. **Minimal canvas, playful moments** — Restrained layout and type; delight through motion and interaction where it reinforces hierarchy or feedback.
3. **Dev + hiring audience** — Fast to scan, easy to read long posts, portfolio and blog equally credible.
4. **Notion × Cursor** — Structured, calm pages with product-grade polish and developer-appropriate detail (spacing, focus states, subtle motion).
5. **Accessibility is non-negotiable** — Meet **WCAG 2.1 AA**; essential information and actions work without relying on color or animation alone.
